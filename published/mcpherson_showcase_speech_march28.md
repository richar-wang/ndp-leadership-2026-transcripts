## Campaign Video

**Heather McPherson's mother:**

Heather was always a fighter. She and her brothers grew up knowing hard work means something. You show up. You treat people with respect. You take care of your family. You look out for your neighbours. You don't quit. One thing about my daughter Heather — she doesn't quit.

Our table was always full. There was always an extra chair if someone needed it, and if there wasn't, you just squeezed in a little closer. That's how we did things. Heather grew up with that. She learned early that there's always enough when you share, and that no one should be left standing on the outside. I watched her carry that her whole life, in everything she does. She's still making room for people.

**Heather McPherson's daughter:**

My mom worked hard and always supported everything my brother and I did. Early mornings at the pool, late nights on the ice — she was there. And she'd make breakfast for half the neighborhood who would just show up, because that's who she is.

Ma mère se bat pour nous depuis toujours, et depuis que je suis petite, je sais qu'elle se bat pour tout le monde.

She doesn't know how to stop. It drives me a little crazy, but it's also why I am so proud of her.

**Supporter:**

The Liberal Party, the Conservative Party, refuses to talk about human rights in Yemen, because both of them are complicit in the selling of arms to the regime that is propping up that war. It is not alright that neither one of them will talk about human rights in Palestine, that children in Palestine are being murdered, and that neither of these parties will talk about that.

**Supporter:**

I've known Heather a very long time. Before any of this. Before she was a member of Parliament. Before the campaigns where she was knocking on every door in the constituency. In the movement, Heather was building something in Alberta that nobody thought was possible. She was bringing people together who didn't always agree. She was building consensus among them, and she was inspiring cooperation. And in Alberta, Heather was doing the patient, meticulous work of creating change. She's been doing this work her whole life, and Parliament just gave her a bigger room.

**Supporter:**

Heather is the perfect woman for this job. I've watched Heather fight for fairness and human rights across Canada and around the world. I've watched her fight for Palestinians, Ukrainians, Afghans, and Sudanese. And I've seen her bring people together to find solutions, to use her seat in the House of Commons to effect real change, and to push our caucus, our party, and the government to take action no one thought possible. Heather never backs down from a challenge, and she refuses to turn her back on injustice, no matter where it occurs.

## Amanda Freistadt, trade unionist and party organizer

Bonjour, tout le monde. My name is Amanda Freistadt. I use she and her pronouns.

I believe in action over promises. I've spent 20 years as a builder in this party — in Saskatchewan as treasurer, as a federal counselor, as a labour VP, EPC chair, CRO, CEDA; in Alberta; and as a trade unionist, from health care to telecom to retail to heavy equipment. I've been a union staffer who builds action on shop floors, and I've done it across this entire country.

I support Heather because she acts. It's action. She acts for the workers, and the workers.

Heather shows up on the picket lines in the dark and in the cold. She shows up in the House of Commons tabling and supporting legislation to protect and expand workers' rights. And she understands that solidarity means more than talk — it's taking our issues to Parliament and fighting for us there.

Heather is a builder. And with Heather as leader, we will keep building — the whole party, and for all working Canadians.

At the launch of her campaign, the president of the Edmonton CUPW local endorsed her from the stage and said, "I support those who support the working class, and Heather has my support." I could not have said it better myself.

*\[applause\]*

## Heather McPherson, candidate for NDP leadership

Thank you! Thank you! Thank you! Thank you, thank you, everyone!

From the beginning, this campaign has been about building a bigger table and making sure that there is a place for everyday Canadians to have a voice where laws are passed and where decisions are made. That's why workers and farmers came together to build our party. And it's why we need to rebuild it.

Because right now — right now across the country — people are struggling. Working people are feeling squeezed from every direction. We're seeing scapegoating, more division, too many people carrying more than their share of the weight.

Conservative governments are attacking public health care like we haven't seen since Tommy Douglas and the NDP built it. They are trampling on workers' rights. They are rolling back the rights of the most vulnerable.

*\[cheers\]*

That's right. And Donald Trump is a threat to our economy, to our sovereignty, and to our planet.

Now is not the time for us to shrink even more. We haven't been losing support because our values are wrong. We just haven't always connected as well as we could. People just want to know who's on their side.

And this weekend — this weekend — together, New Democrats can show Canadians that we have their back.

*\[applause\]*

You know, I didn't grow up in a political family. My mom and dad worked hard — they're here today. But they did teach us to care for others, and that you can always make more room at your table. And that idea stuck with me, you know, through 20 years of building coalitions with people that we don't always agree with, fighting for human rights, showing up for those people that the system has left behind.

In 2008, when I was door-knocking with Linda Duncan in Edmonton-Strathcona —

*\[cheers\]*

There it is! We earned support from people who hadn't voted NDP before. And when I ran in 2019, every news camera went to the Conservative campaign office to celebrate their victory. And when the results came in, they had to rush right back to our campaign headquarters.

*\[cheers and applause\]*

And they learned something. They learned something that everyone in this room knows: never underestimate New Democrats.

Ensemble, on peut faire encore tellement plus — pour les familles, pour les travailleuses et les travailleurs, pour tout le monde.

Medicare, workers' rights — we can do so much more for families, for workers, and for everyone. Medicare, workers' rights, employment insurance, pension protection, human rights — so many of the things that make this country great, New Democrats built. That's why it's on us to defend them.

You know, that's why holding a membership card should mean much more than getting fundraising emails. It is a symbol — it's a symbol of every one of your role in rebuilding our party, of winning power back for the Canadians that are depending on us. Because it's a political party. That's our job.

Everyone deserves a home — not a tax loophole, not a vessel for corporate profiteering. A home.

Reconciliation is a path that we need to actively take, not just talk about.

We need to fix and expand public health care — dental care, mental health care, and pharmacare.

Workers' rights need to be strengthened, not abandoned. And we need to protect public services, not cut them.

Our future needs to be built here, with Canadian materials and Canadian jobs, and a plan to address the climate crisis that workers can support, because they are part of the solution — not ignored, not patronized, and not treated like part of the problem.

*\[applause\]*

And Canada must — Canada must — be a voice for peace, for human rights, for international law. For standing with Ukraine. For standing for justice in Palestine.

*\[cheers\]*

Here's what New Democrats stand for: a good job. A home you can afford. Head-to-toe health care that you don't have to pay for. Ever. And a government — a government — that serves you, not the billionaires that these guys keep letting off the hook. And we need to be building a cleaner, brighter future for our kids, with more hope, with more opportunity for everyone.

En Abitibi, partout au pays, le Québec est au centre de ce travail. Je suis là, et je vais rester là.

We have to get back to every corner of our country. Quebec is in the middle of this. I'm here, and I will stay here. I will be there, and together we will win for people.

À l'écoute, et ensemble, on va gagner pour les gens.

*\[applause\]*

You know, once, after a tough election loss, Tommy Douglas quoted an old Scottish ballad: "I am hurt, but I am not slain. Let me lie down and bleed a while, but then I'll rise and fight again."

Friends, we are already fighting again. Right now, in British Columbia and Manitoba, we are governing. We are the official opposition in the Yukon, in Alberta, in Saskatchewan, in Ontario, in Nova Scotia. Across the country, our values are winning. And we can choose to ignore or even oppose that success, or we can build from it, together. That's our solidarity. That is our strength.

So if you're ready to rebuild our party, I'm asking for your support. Let's leave this convention re-energized. And together, like those who have built and rebuilt the NDP before us — conversation by conversation, community by community, riding by riding — let's make sure everyone has a seat at this table.

I have a plan. I have a record. And I'm already doing the work.

Thank you.

*\[cheers and applause\]*
